 I took a trip with my wife and my family in July to Anchorage, Alaska, and then the Kenai Peninsula for a week of fishing and some natural site-seeing.  Alaska is a humongous place, and while we didn't see many of the famous natural sites, we did get great taste of the summer fishing season.  

 Visiting Alaska was a first-time experience for me, and the furtherest North I've ever been.  This September I'll be smashing that record with a trip to Iceland and Norway.  On my next trip to Alaska, I'd love to make it out to Denali National Park and visit other natural sites.

 Below is a satellite image of the Kenai Peninsula, which is located to the south of Anchorage, and forms the eastern portion of the Cook Inlet.

 <img src="{{site.baseurl}}/assets/img/kenai.jpeg" />


### Hot Takes

One of the most fascinating things about Alaska to me was how it never truly got dark at night.  Living in Portland for the past 4 years, I had gotten used to the sun going down around 9 in midsummer due to the higher latitude, but this was an entirely different type of thing.

 Also, like being close to the equator, the full sun warmed you up quickly, despite it being 60 degrees Fahrenheit.  Winter of course means the opposite case, with nights that seemingly never end.

### Day 0 / 1 - Travel, Seward, Soldotna

We took a red-eye flight from Portland to Anchorage, and spent the night near the airport.  The next morning we picked up our rental car, and left ahead of my family to drive around Anchorage briefly before heading down Alaska Route 1.  

On Route 1 we drove along the Cook Inlet in the direction of the Kenai Peninsula with an intent on arriving at Soldotna by the end of the day.  Along the way we made a detour to Seward, which is the city immediately adjacent to the [Kenai Fjords National Park](https://www.nps.gov/kefj/index.htm).  In Seward we stopped at the National Park visitor center and grabbed some dinner before continuing on to Soldotna.

### Day 2 - Float Plane to Wolverine Creek

On the second day the action stepped into high gear, with the eight of us loading into float planes on a nearby lake and flying across the Cook Inlet and landing in Redoubt Bay, switching to a flat-bottomed boat and driving to [Wolverine Creek](http://www.adfg.alaska.gov/index.cfm?adfg=viewinglocations.wolverinecreekredoubtbay) to fish and maybe catch a chance at seeing some wildlife.

The float plane ride was probably my favorite experience of the whole trip. I had never ridden on a plane that small, or taken off and landed on water.  It was an overcast day, but I feel that it contributed perfectly to the experience.

Once we arrived at Wolverine Creek, it wasn't 20 minutes before a female Brown Bear and her cub arrived on scene to hunt for fish.  They revisited many times during the several hours that we were fishing, and we even caught sight of a solitary Black Bear
